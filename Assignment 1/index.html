Catalog about Birds

Blue Jay

Blue Jays are birds that belong to the Corvidae family, being easily recognizable by their blue-colored feathers. They are medium sized birds (about 22 - 30 cm) and are native to North America. The crest of blue jays, identified by a noticeable cluster of feathers atop their heads, is one of their most striking characteristics. The black markings on a blue jay's face adds great color and detail to their overall look. Because of their great adaptability, blue jays can be found in a wide range of environments, such as parks, cities, and forests. Their diet consists of seeds, nuts, insects, tiny vertebrates, and even the eggs of other birds. . Their thick, powerful bills are ideal for breaking open seeds and nuts.

Bald Eagle

The majestic and well-known bald eagle is revered in American history and culture as the national bird and symbol of the country. The remarkable look of adult bald eagles makes them clearly identifiable. Their body is dark brown, but they have a characteristic white head and tail. Their enormous wingspan, which frequently reaches up to 7 feet (approximately 2.1 meters), helps with their proficiency as aviators. The mighty bald eagle's beak and talons are designed to catch and hold their prey, such as fish, which is the main portion of their diet. Because of this, bald eagles are usually found near large bodies of open water, with their nests nearby located up high on trees.

Barn Swallow

Barn Swallows are easily recognizable by their unique markings and graceful aerial acrobatics. These nimble birds inhabit a variety of environments, including farmlands, open spaces, and meadows. They are well-known for their brilliant feathers and exceptional flying skills. Barn swallows are petite birds with a slender physique. Their eye-catching cobalt-blue upper parts contribute to their easy recognition. While both male and female barn swallows display comparable markings, the female's colors are generally more understated. Not only are barn swallows recognized for their colors, but also for their skill in constructing cup-shaped nests using mud, typically affixing them beneath various structures like barns, bridges, or other human-made constructions. These nests are furnished with soft materials such as feathers and grass, creating a cozy and protected space for their offspring.

Mallard

Mallards are well-known ducks, appreciated for their delightful looks and ability to thrive in diverse aquatic environments. These medium-sized waterfowl are present in North America, Europe, Asia, and some regions of North Africa. Male ballards have green heads while female ballards have a layer of feathers that is speckled with the color brown. Mallards are about 50 - 65 cm long with a wingspan of about 81 - 98 cm. Mallards exhibit great adaptability, frequently observed in a range of aquatic settings such as ponds, lakes, rivers, and marshes. Their omnivorous eating habits encompass aquatic plants, insects, small fish, and even grains. Their notable vocalizations include distinctive quacks, with male mallards emitting a softer sound, while the females produce a louder and more characteristic quack.
